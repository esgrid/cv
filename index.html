Esgrid Sikahall

Software Engineer and Philosopher of Science and Religion, working at CodeClan.

I am a Software Development instructor at CodeClan, the first digital skills academy in Edinburgh, Scotland.

I was a Mathematics Lecturer for four years in Guatemala (Universidad del Valle) before moving to study an MSc in Science and Religion at the University of Edinburgh. After that, I did a brief stint as a Mathematics Teacher in Scarborough (UTC), and then my wife and I moved back to Edinburgh to start my doctorate under Professor Mark Harris, a Physicist interested in Biblical Interpretation, (Quantum) Physics and Theology. Whilst a doctoral student I delivered Introduction to Programming courses for humanities postgraduate students, academic staff and non-academic staff, and I also participated in the first Digital Skills Summer School of the Centre for Data, Culture, and Society of the University of Edinburgh, teaching Data Wrangling and Data Wisualisation in Python. Now, as an Instructor at CodeClan, I enjoy teaching Full-Stack Web Development and in my daydreaming moments I think about Software Engineering and Machine Learning and their philosophical fecundity.
